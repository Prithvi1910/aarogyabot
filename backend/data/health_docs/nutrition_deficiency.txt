NUTRITION, ANAEMIA AND VITAMIN DEFICIENCY GUIDANCE FOR RURAL HEALTHCARE

Nutritional deficiencies are very common in rural India, especially among women, growing children, and pregnant mothers. They develop slowly and are often missed. Eating a balanced, varied diet and using government nutrition programmes (Anganwadi, mid-day meals, iron-folic acid distribution) prevents most of these problems.

1. IRON-DEFICIENCY ANAEMIA
Most Common Cause: Low iron in the diet, blood loss (heavy periods, worm infestation), or pregnancy.
Common Signs: Tiredness, weakness, pale skin, pale inner eyelids and tongue, breathlessness on mild work, dizziness, brittle nails, eating soil or chalk (pica).
Simple Home Care Advice: Eat iron-rich foods daily — green leafy vegetables (spinach, drumstick leaves), jaggery, dates, groundnuts, beans, and pulses. Eat a vitamin-C food (lemon, amla, guava, orange) with meals to absorb iron better. Avoid drinking tea or coffee with meals as they block iron absorption.
Safe Medicine: Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) tablets are distributed free at PHCs and Anganwadis; take them as advised. Deworming once helps if worms are the cause.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Severe breathlessness, chest pain, swelling of feet, very fast heartbeat, fainting, or anaemia during pregnancy.

2. VITAMIN A DEFICIENCY
Most Common Cause: Lack of vitamin A foods, common in young children.
Common Signs: Difficulty seeing in dim light (night blindness), dry eyes, white foamy spots on the white of the eye, frequent infections.
Simple Home Care Advice: Give yellow and orange fruits and vegetables (carrot, pumpkin, ripe mango, papaya), green leafy vegetables, egg, and milk.
Safe Medicine: High-dose Vitamin A is given at PHCs and Anganwadis under the national programme for children aged 9 months to 5 years.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Night blindness, cloudy or damaged cornea (this can cause permanent blindness and needs urgent care).

3. VITAMIN B12 AND FOLATE DEFICIENCY
Most Common Cause: Diets with little or no animal foods, poor absorption.
Common Signs: Tiredness, weakness, tingling or numbness in hands and feet, sore tongue, poor memory.
Simple Home Care Advice: Eat milk, curd, egg, and (if non-vegetarian) fish and meat. For folate, eat green leafy vegetables and pulses.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Numbness or weakness that is spreading, trouble walking, or confusion — visit the PHC for testing and supplements.

4. VITAMIN D AND CALCIUM DEFICIENCY
Most Common Cause: Too little sunlight exposure and low milk/calcium intake.
Common Signs: Bone and muscle pain, weakness, in children bowed legs (rickets), delayed walking.
Simple Home Care Advice: Spend 15-20 minutes in morning sunlight on bare arms and face. Eat milk, curd, ragi (finger millet), sesame seeds, and green leafy vegetables.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Bone deformities in children, repeated fractures, or severe bone pain.

5. IODINE DEFICIENCY
Most Common Cause: Eating non-iodised salt.
Common Signs: Swelling at the front of the neck (goitre), tiredness, in children poor growth and learning.
Simple Home Care Advice: Always use iodised salt for cooking. Store salt in a closed, dry container away from heat.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Visible neck swelling, especially in children or pregnant women.

6. PROTEIN-ENERGY MALNUTRITION IN CHILDREN
Most Common Cause: Not enough food, repeated infections, poor feeding during illness.
Common Signs: Low weight for age, thin arms and legs, swelling of feet, loss of appetite, frequent illness, slow growth.
Simple Home Care Advice: Continue breastfeeding up to 2 years. Add energy-rich foods (oil, ghee, groundnut, banana, egg, pulses) to the child's meals. Feed small amounts more often. Keep feeding during illness, including extra after recovery.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: A child with severe thinness, swelling of both feet, or refusal to eat needs urgent referral — visit the PHC or nutrition centre immediately.

PREVENTION SUMMARY: Eat a varied diet with grains, pulses, vegetables, fruits, and milk. Use iodised salt. Take IFA tablets in pregnancy and adolescence. Deworm children every 6 months. Attend Anganwadi growth-monitoring sessions.
