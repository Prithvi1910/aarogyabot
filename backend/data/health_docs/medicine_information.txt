MEDICINE INFORMATION FOR DISEASES AND HEALTH CONDITIONS

General Guidance: Always consult a doctor or Primary Health Centre (PHC) before taking medications for severe illnesses. The following information is for general awareness and emergency first-aid only.

1. MALARIA: 
- Medicines: Prescribed antimalarials such as Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT) or Chloroquine.
- Fever Management: Use Paracetamol. Do not take other painkillers without consulting a doctor.

2. DENGUE: 
- Medicines: ONLY Paracetamol for fever and body ache. 
- STRICTLY AVOID: Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Diclofenac, and Nimesulide, as these increase the risk of severe bleeding. 
- Supportive Care: Drink plenty of Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) fluids, coconut water, and juices.

3. TUBERCULOSIS (TB): 
- Medicines: A strict DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course) regimen, typically including Isoniazid, Rifampin, Ethambutol, and Pyrazinamide.
- Important: This must be prescribed by a PHC. Do not take over-the-counter cough syrups to suppress the cough. Complete the full course of medicines.

4. TYPHOID: 
- Medicines: Prescribed antibiotics like Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, or Ceftriaxone. 
- Fever Management: Use Paracetamol. Complete the full antibiotic course to prevent resistance.

5. CHIKUNGUNYA: 
- Medicines: Paracetamol for fever. Mild painkillers under medical supervision for severe joint pain.
- Important: Do not take strong painkillers without a prescription.

6. CHOLERA AND SEVERE DIARRHEA: 
- Medicines: The most critical treatment is severe rehydration using Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS). 
- Additional: Zinc supplements for children. Antibiotics like Doxycycline or Azithromycin may be prescribed in severe cases.
- Avoid: Anti-diarrheal medicines (like Loperamide) should generally be avoided unless prescribed.

7. HEPATITIS A: 
- Medicines: No specific antiviral medicines. Treatment involves supportive care, adequate rest, and hydration.
- Avoid: Strictly avoid alcohol and any heavy medications (like unnecessary painkillers) that can stress the inflamed liver.

8. LEPTOSPIROSIS: 
- Medicines: Antibiotics such as Doxycycline or Penicillin, prescribed by a doctor. Early treatment is crucial.

9. COMMON COLDS & MILD FEVER: 
- Medicines: Paracetamol for fever or body ache. Warm fluids and steam inhalation. 
- Avoid: Do not use antibiotics for viral colds.

10. MINOR CUTS AND WOUNDS: 
- Medicines: Antiseptic creams (like Povidone-Iodine). 
- Additional: Tetanus toxoid injection if the wound is deep or caused by a dirty/rusty object.
