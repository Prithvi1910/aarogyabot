Skin, Eye, and Ear Health: Common Conditions

Common Skin Conditions and Rashes
Skin issues are highly prevalent and require proper hygiene and treatment.
1. Scabies: An itchy skin condition caused by microscopic mites that burrow into the skin. It causes intense itching, especially at night, and a pimple-like rash. It spreads easily through close physical contact.
2. Ringworm: A common fungal infection that causes a red, itchy, circular rash with clearer skin in the middle. It spreads through contact with infected people, animals, or contaminated surfaces.
3. Impetigo: A highly contagious bacterial skin infection causing red sores that quickly rupture, ooze for a few days, and then form a yellow-brown crust. It is common in children.
4. Allergic Rashes: Rashes triggered by contact with allergens. They are usually itchy, raised, and localized to the area of contact.
5. Dengue Rash: Unlike itchy skin rashes, a dengue rash is usually a non-itchy flat red rash that appears 3-4 days after the onset of high fever, covering most of the body. It requires immediate medical evaluation.

Common Eye Infections
Eye health is crucial, and early treatment prevents permanent damage.
- Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye): Inflammation of the conjunctiva, causing redness, irritation, watering, and discharge in one or both eyes. It can be viral, bacterial, or allergic. Prevent spread by washing hands and avoiding sharing towels.
- Trachoma: A contagious bacterial infection of the eye. Repeated infections cause scarring of the inner eyelids, leading to the eyelashes turning inward and rubbing against the cornea (trichiasis), which can cause irreversible blindness if untreated. Practice clean face-washing habits.

Ear Infections and Hearing Issues in Children
Ear infections are common in children and can affect their development.
- Otitis Media: An infection of the middle ear, often following a cold. Symptoms include ear pain, fluid drainage from the ear, fever, and temporary hearing loss. Children may tug at their ears or be unusually irritable.
- Prevention and Care: Keep ears clean and dry. Never insert sharp objects, ear buds, or oils into the ear canal. If ear discharge lasts for more than a few days, consult a healthcare provider. Untreated ear infections can lead to permanent hearing loss, affecting speech and language acquisition in young children.
