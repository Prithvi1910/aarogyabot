COMMON DIGESTIVE AND URINARY CONDITIONS FOR RURAL HEALTHCARE

Stomach, bowel, and urinary complaints are very common. Most are mild and improve with simple diet changes and hydration, but certain warning signs need a PHC visit.

1. ACIDITY AND HEARTBURN (GASTRITIS / GERD)
Most Common Cause: Spicy or oily food, irregular meals, tea/coffee, tobacco, alcohol, or stress.
Common Signs: Burning in the upper stomach or chest, sour taste, belching, bloating, worse when lying down or on an empty stomach.
Simple Home Care Advice: Eat smaller, regular meals. Avoid spicy, oily, and very sour foods, tea, coffee, tobacco, and alcohol. Do not lie down right after eating. Raise the head while sleeping.
Safe Medicine: Over-the-counter antacids can give short-term relief. For frequent symptoms, the PHC may advise an acid-reducing medicine.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Severe or constant pain, black or bloody stools, vomiting blood, difficulty swallowing, unexplained weight loss, or chest pain with sweating (which could be a heart problem).

2. CONSTIPATION
Most Common Cause: Low fibre, not enough water, low physical activity, or holding the urge.
Common Signs: Hard, infrequent, or difficult-to-pass stools.
Simple Home Care Advice: Drink more water. Eat fibre — whole grains, vegetables, fruits (papaya, banana), and soaked figs. Walk and stay active. Go to the toilet when you feel the urge.
Safe Medicine: A simple stool-softening fibre supplement (like Isabgol/psyllium husk) with plenty of water can help.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Constipation with severe pain, vomiting, blood in stool, or a sudden lasting change in bowel habit.

3. ACUTE DIARRHOEA AND FOOD POISONING
Most Common Cause: Contaminated food or water, infections.
Common Signs: Loose or watery stools, stomach cramps, sometimes vomiting or mild fever.
Simple Home Care Advice: The most important step is preventing dehydration. Drink ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts) after each loose stool, plus clean fluids, coconut water, and thin rice gruel. Keep eating small, simple meals; continue feeding children and breastfeeding.
Safe Medicine: ORS for fluid loss. Zinc supplements for 14 days help children recover. Avoid anti-diarrhoeal medicines like Loperamide unless a doctor advises.
ORS PREPARATION: Dissolve one ORS packet in 1 litre of clean water. If no packet, mix six level teaspoons of sugar and half a teaspoon of salt in 1 litre of clean water.
Red-Flag / Emergency: Blood in stool, high fever, signs of dehydration (no urine for 8 hours, sunken eyes, extreme weakness, no tears in a child), or diarrhoea that does not improve in 2 days — visit the PHC urgently.

4. WORM INFESTATION (INTESTINAL WORMS)
Most Common Cause: Eating with dirty hands, contaminated food or water, or walking barefoot on soil.
Common Signs: Stomach pain, poor appetite, weakness, anaemia, itching around the anus, or worms visible in stool.
Simple Home Care Advice: Wash hands with soap before eating and after toilet. Wash fruits and vegetables. Wear footwear. Drink safe water.
Safe Medicine: Deworming with Albendazole is given free at schools and Anganwadis, usually every 6 months for children. The whole family may be advised to take it.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Severe abdominal pain, ongoing weight loss, or signs of anaemia.

5. URINARY TRACT INFECTION (UTI)
Most Common Cause: Bacteria entering the urinary tract; more common in women and during pregnancy.
Common Signs: Burning while passing urine, passing urine often, urgency, lower abdominal pain, cloudy or strong-smelling urine.
Simple Home Care Advice: Drink plenty of clean water to flush the system. Keep the genital area clean and dry. Do not hold urine for long.
Safe Medicine: Fluids and Paracetamol for discomfort. A UTI usually needs prescribed antibiotics — visit the PHC, especially if pregnant.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Fever with chills, back or side pain, blood in urine, vomiting, or symptoms in a pregnant woman or child — visit the PHC promptly.

6. PILES (HAEMORRHOIDS)
Most Common Cause: Long-standing constipation and straining.
Common Signs: Pain, itching, or bright red bleeding while passing stool; a lump near the anus.
Simple Home Care Advice: Treat constipation — more water and fibre. Sit in a tub of warm water (sitz bath) for relief. Avoid straining and prolonged sitting.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Heavy or repeated bleeding, severe pain, or a hard painful lump — visit the PHC.

PREVENTION SUMMARY: Drink safe (boiled or filtered) water, wash hands with soap before eating and after the toilet, eat freshly cooked food, wash fruits and vegetables, eat enough fibre, and use clean toilets to prevent most digestive and urinary infections.
