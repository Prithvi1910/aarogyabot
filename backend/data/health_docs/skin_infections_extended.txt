COMMON SKIN INFECTIONS AND CONDITIONS FOR RURAL HEALTHCARE

Skin problems are very common where there is heat, humidity, crowding, and limited clean water. Most are mild and treatable, but spreading infection, fever, or wounds that will not heal need a PHC visit.

1. SCABIES (ITCH MITE)
Most Common Cause: Tiny mites spread by close skin contact and shared clothing or bedding.
Common Signs: Intense itching, worse at night, with small red bumps and burrow lines between fingers, wrists, waist, and groin. Often several family members itch.
Simple Home Care Advice: Wash all clothes and bedding in hot water and dry in strong sunlight. Treat all family members at the same time even if they have no itch yet.
Safe Medicine: Permethrin 5% cream is the usual treatment, applied to the whole body from neck down as directed at the PHC. Calamine lotion or an antihistamine (Cetirizine) can ease itching.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Pus, spreading redness, fever, or itching that does not improve after treatment.

2. RINGWORM AND FUNGAL INFECTIONS
Most Common Cause: Fungus, common in skin folds, groin, and feet, worsened by sweat and tight clothes.
Common Signs: Round, red, itchy patches with a raised scaly border; or itching and cracking between the toes (athlete's foot).
Simple Home Care Advice: Keep the area clean and dry. Wear loose cotton clothes. Do not share towels or clothing. Dry skin folds well after bathing.
Safe Medicine: Antifungal creams such as Clotrimazole, applied as directed, usually for a few weeks. Continue for some days after it clears to prevent return.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Large or widespread patches, infection of the scalp or nails, or no improvement after 2-3 weeks.

3. BOILS AND ABSCESSES
Most Common Cause: Bacterial infection of a hair root or skin gland.
Common Signs: A painful, red, swollen lump that may fill with pus.
Simple Home Care Advice: Apply a warm, clean compress several times a day to help it drain naturally. Keep it clean and do not squeeze or burst it forcefully.
Safe Medicine: Antiseptic such as Povidone-Iodine on the area. Paracetamol for pain.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: A large or very painful boil, boils with fever, boils on the face or spine, spreading redness, or repeated boils (may need drainage or treatment for diabetes).

4. IMPETIGO (INFECTED SORES, COMMON IN CHILDREN)
Most Common Cause: Bacterial infection, often on the face, that spreads by touch.
Common Signs: Red sores that ooze and form yellow-brown (honey-coloured) crusts, often around the nose and mouth.
Simple Home Care Advice: Gently clean the crusts with clean water and soap. Keep the child's nails short and discourage scratching. Wash hands and avoid sharing towels.
Safe Medicine: Antiseptic cleaning; the PHC may prescribe an antibiotic cream or tablets.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Spreading sores, fever, or sores that worsen — visit the PHC.

5. PRICKLY HEAT (HEAT RASH)
Most Common Cause: Blocked sweat glands in hot, humid weather.
Common Signs: Tiny itchy red bumps on the neck, chest, and back.
Simple Home Care Advice: Stay cool, wear loose cotton clothing, bathe with cool water, and keep the skin dry. Calamine lotion soothes itching.
Red-Flag Signs to See a Doctor: Pus, fever, or the rash becoming painful and infected.

6. ALLERGIC SKIN RASH AND HIVES
Most Common Cause: Reaction to food, medicine, insect bites, plants, or dust.
Common Signs: Raised itchy welts (hives) or red itchy patches that may come and go.
Simple Home Care Advice: Avoid the suspected trigger. Apply a cool compress.
Safe Medicine: An antihistamine such as Cetirizine reduces itching.
Red-Flag / Emergency: Swelling of the lips, tongue, or throat, difficulty breathing, or dizziness is a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) — go to hospital immediately.

7. HEAD LICE
Most Common Cause: Tiny insects spread by head-to-head contact, common in school children.
Common Signs: Itchy scalp and small white eggs (nits) stuck to hair near the scalp.
Simple Home Care Advice: Comb wet hair with a fine-toothed lice comb daily. Wash bedding and combs. Treat affected family members together.
Safe Medicine: A medicated lice lotion (such as Permethrin) from the PHC or pharmacy.

PREVENTION SUMMARY: Bathe regularly with soap and clean water, keep skin dry, wear clean loose clothing, do not share towels, combs, or clothes, keep nails short, and wash hands often.
