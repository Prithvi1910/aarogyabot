SAFE USE OF COMMON OVER-THE-COUNTER MEDICINES AND HOME REMEDIES

This guide covers safe, commonly available remedies for MILD symptoms only. For severe illness, pregnancy, young children, or any prescription medicine (antibiotics, antimalarials, strong painkillers), always consult a doctor at the nearest Primary Health Centre (PHC). Never take leftover or other people's prescription medicines.

1. PARACETAMOL (for fever and pain)
Used For: Fever, headache, body ache, mild pain. It is the safest first choice for fever, including in dengue.
Safe Use: Take with water after food. Do not exceed the dose on the packet. Leave at least 4-6 hours between doses. Do not combine multiple medicines that all contain Paracetamol.
Caution: Avoid extra doses if you have liver disease or drink alcohol. For children, use the syrup by weight as advised at the PHC.

2. ORAL REHYDRATION SALTS (ORS) (for fluid loss)
Used For: Diarrhoea, vomiting, dehydration, heat exhaustion.
Preparation: Dissolve one ORS packet in 1 litre of clean water. If no packet is available, mix six level teaspoons of sugar and half a teaspoon of salt in 1 litre of clean water. Sip frequently, especially after each loose stool.
Caution: Make a fresh solution each day. Continue feeding and breastfeeding. Zinc supplements for 14 days help children with diarrhoea.

3. CETIRIZINE (antihistamine, for allergy and itching)
Used For: Allergic rashes, hives, itching, runny nose from allergy, mild insect-bite reactions.
Safe Use: Usually taken once daily as on the packet. It may cause mild drowsiness.
Caution: Does not treat infections. Seek urgent care for swelling of lips/tongue/throat or breathing difficulty (severe allergy).

4. ANTACIDS (for acidity)
Used For: Short-term relief of heartburn and acidity.
Safe Use: Take as directed for occasional symptoms.
Caution: If you need it often, see the PHC. Black stools, vomiting blood, or chest pain with sweating need urgent care.

5. POVIDONE-IODINE / ANTISEPTIC (for minor wounds)
Used For: Cleaning minor cuts, scrapes, and grazes after washing with clean water and soap.
Safe Use: Apply a thin layer and cover with a clean bandage.
Caution: Deep, large, or dirty wounds and animal bites need medical care and a tetanus injection.

6. CLOTRIMAZOLE CREAM (antifungal)
Used For: Ringworm and other mild fungal skin infections.
Safe Use: Apply to the clean, dry area as directed, and continue for some days after it clears.
Caution: See the PHC for large, scalp, or nail infections, or if there is no improvement in 2-3 weeks.

7. ALBENDAZOLE (deworming)
Used For: Routine deworming, given free at schools and Anganwadis, usually every 6 months for children.
Safe Use: Take as advised in the programme.
Caution: Pregnant women in the first three months should consult a doctor first.

8. IRON AND FOLIC ACID (IFA) TABLETS
Used For: Preventing and treating anaemia, especially in adolescents, women, and pregnancy. Distributed free at PHCs and Anganwadis.
Safe Use: Take with a vitamin-C food (lemon, amla) for better absorption; avoid taking with tea or coffee. Stools may turn dark, which is harmless.
Caution: Keep away from children, as overdose is dangerous.

MEDICINES TO AVOID WITHOUT A DOCTOR
- Antibiotics: Do not use for ordinary colds, cough, or viral fever. Misuse causes resistance. Only take antibiotics prescribed by a doctor, and finish the full course.
- Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Diclofenac, Nimesulide: Avoid in dengue or suspected dengue (bleeding risk); use Paracetamol instead. Avoid strong painkillers on an empty stomach or with stomach ulcers.
- Steroid creams and tonics: Do not use on the skin or take without medical advice.
- Cough suppressants: Do not use to silence a long-standing cough, which may be TB — get it checked.

GENERAL SAFETY: Check the expiry date, store medicines in a cool dry place away from children, never share prescription medicines, and tell the doctor about pregnancy, allergies, and all medicines you already take.
