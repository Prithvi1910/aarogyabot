Maternal and Child Health: A Comprehensive Guide for Families

Maternal Health: Pregnancy Warning Signs
Pregnancy is a critical period for maternal health. Expectant mothers must monitor their health closely and seek immediate medical attention at the nearest primary health center or hospital if they experience any of the following warning signs:
1. Severe vaginal bleeding or spotting.
2. High fever with or without chills.
3. Severe, persistent headaches or blurred vision (signs of preeclampsia).
4. Sudden swelling of the face, hands, or feet.
5. Severe abdominal pain.
6. Reduced fetal movement (baby moving less than usual).
7. Convulsions or fits.
8. Premature leakage of fluid from the vagina.

Child Health: Vaccination Schedule (0 to 5 Years)
Immunization protects children from life-threatening diseases. The essential vaccination schedule includes:
- At Birth: BCG (Tuberculosis vaccine), OPV-0 (Oral Polio Vaccine), and Hepatitis B birth dose.
- 6 Weeks: Pentavalent 1 (DPT + Hepatitis B + Hib), OPV 1, Rotavirus Vaccine (RVV) 1, and Fractional IPV (Inactivated Polio Vaccine) 1.
- 10 Weeks: Pentavalent 2, OPV 2, and RVV 2.
- 14 Weeks: Pentavalent 3, OPV 3, RVV 3, and Fractional IPV 2.
- 9 to 12 Months: MR (Measles-Rubella) 1st dose, Vitamin A 1st dose, and PCV Booster.
- 16 to 24 Months: MR 2nd dose, DPT Booster 1, OPV Booster, and Vitamin A doses (every 6 months up to 5 years).
- 5 Years: DPT Booster 2.
Adhering to this national immunization schedule is vital to build immunity against infectious diseases.

Malnutrition in Children
Malnutrition occurs when a child does not receive the right amount of nutrients. Key warning signs include:
1. Weight-for-age or height-for-age significantly lower than normal growth standards.
2. Muscle wasting, visible ribs, and thin limbs.
3. Swollen abdomen, face, and legs (edema, indicative of severe acute malnutrition or Kwashiorkor).
4. Dry, thinning hair that changes color (reddish-yellow).
5. Persistent fatigue, lethargy, and lack of interest in play.
6. Frequent illnesses due to weakened immunity.
7. Irritability and developmental delays.
Children showing these signs must be referred to a Nutrition Rehabilitation Centre (NRC) immediately.

Postnatal Care Basics
Postnatal care refers to the care of the mother and her newborn baby during the first six weeks after birth. Key aspects include:
- Maternal Care: Adequate rest, nutritious diet rich in iron and calcium, and monitoring for postpartum hemorrhage, infection, or depression.
- Newborn Care: Exclusive breastfeeding for the first six months (no water, honey, or other milk). Keep the baby warm (Kangaroo Mother Care). Ensure proper hygiene of the umbilical cord stump (keep it clean and dry). Watch for warning signs in the newborn: high fever, difficulty breathing, yellow skin/eyes (jaundice), poor feeding, or lethargy.
