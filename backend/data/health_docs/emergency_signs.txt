CRITICAL EMERGENCY SIGNS AND IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

Certain symptoms represent life-threatening medical emergencies. In these situations, the patient must be transferred immediately to the nearest hospital or emergency department. Do not wait to see if symptoms improve, and do not attempt self-treatment at home.

1. CARDIOVASCULAR AND RESPIRATORY EMERGENCIES
- Severe Chest Pain: Sudden, crushing chest pain or pressure that may spread to the left arm, neck, jaw, or back, often accompanied by sweating, shortness of breath, and nausea. This is a primary indicator of a potential heart attack.
- Severe Difficulty Breathing: Inability to speak in full sentences, gasping for air, chest retractions (skin pulling in between ribs), wheezing, or bluish lips/fingernails.

2. NEUROLOGICAL AND SYSTEMIC EMERGENCIES
- Loss of Consciousness: Fainting and not waking up within a minute, being completely unresponsive, or exhibiting confusion and sudden difficulty speaking or moving one side of the body (signs of a stroke).
- Seizures: Convulsions, uncontrolled shaking, or fits, particularly if they last longer than a few minutes or occur repeatedly without regaining consciousness.

3. TRAUMA AND TOXICITY
- Severe Bleeding: Uncontrolled bleeding from any wound that does not stop even after applying firm, direct pressure for 10-15 minutes.
- Suspected Poisoning: Ingestion or inhalation of pesticides, chemical fertilizers, household cleaners, or poisonous plants, or snakebites and scorpion stings.

4. PREGNANCY AND OBSTETRIC EMERGENCIES
- Severe Bleeding during Pregnancy: Any vaginal bleeding during pregnancy, which can signal placental complications.
- Preeclampsia/Eclampsia Signs: A severe, persistent headache, sudden swelling of the face and hands, blurred vision, or seizures (fits) in a pregnant woman. These require immediate medical intervention to save both mother and baby.
